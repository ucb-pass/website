Contemporary Issues of Pilipin@-Americans DeCal

Filipino-American history dates back to 1587, when Filipinos landed in Morro Bay, California. Since then, Filipinos have built and established communities in America and have developed a distinct culture—a Filipino-American culture. Despite being the second largest Asian minority group in the United States, the stories of Filipino-Americans are consistently marginalized and forgotten: accurate accounts of Filipino-American history in the education system is minimal; popular media representation of Filipino-Americans is non-existent; and the struggles that Filipino-Americans have fought (are still fighting) for are ignored. Through an interdisciplinary lens, this course highlights the Filipino-American experience, and more specifically, the myriad of challenges that Filipino-Americans struggle with today and how they relate to the past.

The purpose of this course is to bring awareness of the contemporary issues Filipino-Americans face in society. Awareness will be achieved through educational workshops, dialogue, and active participation in currently existing Filipino-American communities. By the end of this course, students will be able to (1) critically understand contemporary issues facing Filipino-Americans and (2) pass on this knowledge to their communities. Overall, this course hopes to provide a safe space for students to learn about their history, explore their identities, and share their experiences.
